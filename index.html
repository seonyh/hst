About Hopkins Sport Taekwondo

  • Our Club

           Hopkins Sport Taekwondo (HST) was created in the spring of 2002 by twelve black belts who broke off from the ITF style of Taekwondo. The club has grown considerably since then and currently it houses over forty students ranging from white to fourth degree black belt. The US Taekwondo Academy serves as the parent school for HST, under the guidance of Grand Master Se Yong Chang. HST is currently under the instruction of sixth degree black belt Masters Yong Seong (CJ) Chang and Master Rex Nbuta. HST is an active member of the Eastern Collegiate Taekwondo Academy and participating in tournaments is a top priority for our club.

           Any student or affiliate of the Johns Hopkins University may join Hopkins Sport Taekwondo; beginners are welcomed! If you are interested in joining the club feel free to stop by one of our practices (see schedule) or contact us. Don't forget to check us out at the SAC fair in the fall!

  • World Taekwondo Federation

    HST practices World Taekwondo Federation (WTF) style sparring and poomsae. Kukkiwon is the headquarters of the World Taekwondo Federation and is located in Seoul, Korea. The World Taekwondo Federation rules and regulations are the leading form of taekwondo and used during the Olympic Games

  • Class Times

    Practices are held on the Homewood Campus at the Ralph O'Conner Recreation Center.

    General practices are on Mondays and Wednesdays from 9PM to 11PM on the 3rd Floor Multipurpose Rooms. These practices are led by the masters and include conditioning, drills, sparring, and forms.

    New this semester are weekend practices held on Sundays from 1PM to 3PM in the Martial Arts Room. Board members will lead this practice, and it will be a great opportunity for members to get one-on-one attention on specific techniques.

Tournaments

The Hopkins Sport Taekwondo is a yearly member of the Eastern Collegiate Taekwondo Conference. There are a minimum of give tournaments during the Fall and Spring Semester at MIT, Brown University, Cornell University, Priceton University, and the University of Vermont. Each tournament boast around 350 competitors in sparring and poomsae, all of whom are at the collegiate level. Come find out where you rank among other students and see if you can improve at each tournament!

The tournament rules can be found here on the ECTC website.


For Poomsae (Forms) Competition, there are ten poomsae divisions (including both Men and Women):

Division Form
White/Yellow Taeguk 1 or 2
Green Taeguk 3 or 4
Blue Taeguk 5 or 6
Red Taeguk 7 or 8
Black Rotating Black Belt Forms

Contesteants must perform the forms appropriate for their rank as described above or they will be disqualified.

For Sparring Competition, there are three divisions depending on skill level (mens/womens):

Division Size of Team< Duration< Rank<
A-Team 3 Competitors, 2 Alternates 2 rnd of 2 min. with 30 sec rest Blue Belts and up
B-Team 3 Competitors, 2 Alternates 2 rnd of 90 sec with 30 sec rest Red Belts and Below
C-Team 3 Competitors, 2 Alternates 2 rnd of 1 min. with 30 sec rest Green Belts and up
